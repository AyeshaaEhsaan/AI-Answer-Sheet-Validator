Q1 [10 marks]: The Pakistan Movement was a political struggle led mainly by Muslims in British India seeking a separate homeland. It grew from concerns over religious, political, and cultural rights. Leaders like Muhammad Ali Jinnah emphasized autonomy, eventually resulting in the 1947 creation of Pakistan as an independent state.
Q2 [7 marks]: Sir Syed Ahmed Khan advanced the Two Nation Theory by stressing that Muslims and Hindus had distinct identities, histories, and social needs. He promoted modern education for Muslims and urged political representation to protect their interests. His ideas later influenced Muslim nationalism and demands for a separate homeland
Q3 [14 marks]: The Round Table Conferences failed due to deep disagreements between British officials and Indian leaders over constitutional reforms. Communal representation, provincial autonomy, and power distribution remained unresolved. The absence of key voices and lack of binding commitments hindered consensus, leaving major political issues unsettled.
